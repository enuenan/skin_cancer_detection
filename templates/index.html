About Skin Cancer

Skin cancer is the uncontrolled growth of abnormal skin cells. It occurs when DNA damage to skin cells (most often caused by ultraviolet radiation from sunshine or tanning beds) triggers mutations, or genetic defects, that lead the skin cells to multiply rapidly and form malignant tumors.

The two most common types of skin cancer are basal cell carcinoma and squamous cell carcinoma. These are known as non-melanoma skin cancer. A third type of skin cancer is melanoma, which is less common but more dangerous.

Symptoms of skin cancer include a new growth or a sore that doesnâ€™t heal. Skin cancer can develop anywhere on your body, but it is most likely to appear on sun-exposed areas, such as the face, scalp, lips, ears, neck, chest, arms, and hands, and on the legs in women.

To reduce your risk of skin cancer, avoid prolonged sun exposure, use sunscreen, wear protective clothing, and avoid tanning beds. Regular skin examinations by a healthcare professional and self-examinations for any new or unusual growths or changes can help detect skin cancer early.
